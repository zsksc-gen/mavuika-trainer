## 1. What is a CD?

A **CD** refers to a "priming" charged attack where a dash takes place right before the attack comes out. Immediately after, the dash is canceled by the charged attack.

> **What does "priming" mean?** It means the input has been/is being done, but it hasn't been held long enough for a charged attack to actually occur yet.

You are probably familiar with the notation **CCDCDCF 2(CDCDCF)**. Because this is a notation for the game *Genshin Impact*, it makes absolutely zero sense without a proper breakdown.

## 2. Breaking Down the Main Combo: CCDCDCF 2(CDCDCF)

### Phase 1: The Opener (CCD)

The first set of actions starts with **CCD** and not just **CD** because it deals more damage for practically free.

```
[CHARGED ATTACK > CHARGED ATTACK > DASH]

```

* **Tip:** Don't rush the dash. The second charged attack needs a bit of time to start up. If you hit dash too soon, the CD will come out very clunky. Feel out the timing. *(Thanks, Golden!)*

The timing for this is quite lenient. The only essential part is to make sure your **SECOND** charged attack is input during the period in which your **FIRST** charged attack is too early in its animation to be ended by releasing (doing a finisher). This window gives you just enough space to input the second charged attack—exceeding that line without holding down a charged attack will lead straight into a finisher.

### Phase 2: Chaining the CDs (CDCD)

Once you've learned how to CD, you need to chain two of them together in quick succession.

> **Crucial Rule:** The input for CD is exactly as it seems: **CHARGED ATTACK > DASH**. However, you must remember that this **MUST** be executed during an ongoing animation (finisher or charged attack), and you must keep the charged attack held down immediately after doing a CD so you don't accidentally drop into a finisher.

When practicing **CDCD(CF)**, focus strictly on the repeated **CDCD** part. *(Thank you to NovaMaka for explaining this back when it made absolutely zero sense!)*

### Phase 3: The Finisher (CF) & Linking Combos

After the learning curve, you get to relax (kind of). For the proper mono-damage (Mono Pyro, Overload) combo, you wait out the dash cooldown that CDCD gives you by doing a normal **CHARGED ATTACK**. Release the button the exact moment that charged attack touches the enemy, which will naturally lead into a **FINISHER**.

Now for a slightly confusing bit: **canceling OUT of a FINISHER** (the big swing she does when you let go of a charged attack).

To explain it as simply as possible:

* The **FINISHER** animation behaves exactly the same as an **ONGOING CHARGED ATTACK** animation.
* If you do the CD input, the **FINISHER** immediately leads into a **CHARGED ATTACK**.
* From that new **CHARGED ATTACK**, you can CD again.

This gives you the overall action sequence of:


$$\text{CCDCDF} \rightarrow \text{CDCDCF}$$

The **FINISHER** acts the exact same way the first "C" in **CCDCDCF** does, removing the need to start over with a raw charged attack and letting you smoothly connect the entire combo together.

**String all this info together to execute:**


$$\text{CCDCDCF } 2(\text{CDCDCF})$$

The better your execution is, the more likely you are to get every single hit into Mavuika’s burst duration. You need lots of practice to consistently do that, and a frame unlocker helps.

## 3. Alternative Combos

### The 7CDC Combo (Carpal Tunnel Warning)

Her more carpal tunnel-inducing combo is **7cdc** (technically **9cdc**, with 2 sets being outside of ult) or **13/14c**.

This uses an additional bit of tech revolving around pressing the attack button twice quickly after each CD. For some reason, this keeps her in her **CHARGED ATTACK** state. This "handsfree" state allows you to wait for a normal charged attack to connect, and then you can input a CD to essentially loop it for the duration of the combo. This almost doubles your charged attack count compared to simply holding down the button.

* **Recommendation:** I do **NOT** recommend this combo for Overload or Mono-Pyro. It is noticeably worse, harder, way less cool, and a lot more irritating at times.
* **The Singular Use Case:** If the enemy has a permanent Hydro or Cryo aura (basically only useful for **Hydro Tulpa** or **Aeonblight Drake / Local Legends** like Herra), **7CDC** actually allows you to do significantly more damage than the standard combo.

### The Comfort Variant

Moving onto something more useful, there is another variant of Mavuika combos that mixes CDC and CDCDCF to create a combo that is easier than a perfect execution of the main combo, while only sacrificing a small amount of damage:

```
[CDC 2CDCDCF CDCD(CF)]

```

## 4. Advanced Optimization: Early Burst Startup Tech

The final important piece of information is the **early burst startup tech**.

If you begin to input a **CHARGED ATTACK** during the impact frame of Mavuika’s burst, your first charged attack will start much faster than it otherwise would. This saves you important burst duration frames, which can lead to a big bump in overall damage output.
